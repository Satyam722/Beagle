About Beagles

The Beagle is a breed of small hound that is similar in appearance to the much larger foxhound. The Beagle is a scent hound, developed primarily for hunting hare (beagling). With a great sense of smell and superior tracking instinct, the Beagle is employed as detection dog for prohibited agricultural imports and foodstuffs in quarantine around the world. Beagles are intelligent but single-minded and popular pets because of their size, good temper, and lack of inherited health problems.

Beagle Trivia

  1. Origin: Beagles originated in England as hunting dogs.
  2. Size: Beagles typically weigh between 20-25 pounds (9-11 kg).
  3. Temperament: Beagles are known for being friendly, curious, and merry.
  4. Senses: Beagles have an excellent sense of smell and tracking instinct.
  5. Popularity: Beagles are one of the most popular dog breeds in the United States.
  6. Lifespan: The average lifespan of a Beagle is 12-15 years.
  7. Famous Beagle: Snoopy from the Peanuts comic strip is a Beagle.
  8. Colors: Beagles come in various color combinations, most commonly tricolor.
  9. Vocalization: Beagles are known for their distinctive howl or bay.
  10. Exercise Needs: Beagles require regular exercise to prevent obesity.
